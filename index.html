Wildflowers

Wildflowers grow naturally without being planted or seeded and are important contributors to biodiversity. They are extremely important to biodiversity and support ecosystems and pollinators. They provide food and shelter to many species, especially pollinators which then contribute to the agricultural aspects of Georgia's economy.

Mountain Laurel

float:right img src="mountain_laurel_pic.jpg"

Mountain Laurel is a member of the heather family and is frequently found in North Georgia. It can be used for medicine fresh or dry and provides nectar for bees and butterflies in the spring and fruit and seeds for birds in the fall. It is currently on the endangered species list and are threatened by the Weevil.

Georgia Aster

float:right img src="georgia_aster.jpg"

The Georgia Aster is a perennial wildflower that blooms from late September through November. They provide shelter and seeds for many animals in the winter and eveer their stalks can be eaten. They are currentl endandgered due to habitat loss.

Virgina Bluebells

float:right img src="virginia_bluebells.jpg"

Virginia Bluebells are found across North Georgia's woodlands and floodplains. They are ephemeral wildflowers meaning they bloom mid spring and are dormant by the summer.They ar every important for hummingbirds, butterflies, bees, and other insects.
